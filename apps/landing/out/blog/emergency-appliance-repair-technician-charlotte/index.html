Frequently Asked Questions

Is it worth repairing or replacing an appliance?+
A good rule of thumb: if the repair costs more than 50% of the replacement cost, or the appliance is over 10 years old, replacement may be smarter. For newer appliances, repair almost always wins. A technician can give you an honest assessment.
How much does appliance repair cost?+
Diagnostic fees typically run $75–$120. Parts and labor for common repairs: refrigerator $150–$400, washer $100–$350, dryer $100–$300, dishwasher $100–$350, oven $150–$450. Ask for a total estimate before authorizing any repair.
How quickly can an appliance technician come out?+
Many local appliance repair companies offer same-day or next-day service for common appliances. Refrigerators are often prioritized as emergencies. National chains may have scheduling backlogs of 5–14 days — a strong reason to use a local company.
Do appliance repairs come with a warranty?+
Reputable appliance repair companies offer 30–90 day warranties on parts and labor. Ask about the warranty policy before authorizing work — if a company offers no warranty on their repairs, consider it a warning sign.
What brands do appliance technicians service?+
Most independent appliance technicians service all major brands: Whirlpool, GE, LG, Samsung, Bosch, Maytag, Kenmore, KitchenAid, and more. Some brands (Miele, Sub-Zero, Wolf) may require factory-authorized service technicians for warranty work.
