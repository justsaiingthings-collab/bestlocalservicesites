Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new roof cost?+
A full residential roof replacement typically costs $8,000–$25,000 depending on roof size, material (asphalt shingles, metal, tile), and regional labor costs. Repairs for specific sections can range from $500 to $3,000. Always get 3 estimates for any project over $5,000.
How do I find a trustworthy roofing contractor?+
Look for local contractors with verifiable Google reviews, a physical business address, state licensing, and general liability plus workers' comp insurance. Avoid door-to-door storm chasers who show up right after a weather event — they often disappear before problems surface.
Does homeowners insurance cover roof damage?+
Most homeowners insurance covers storm, hail, and wind damage. Age-related deterioration is generally not covered. File your claim quickly after a storm event — most policies have deadlines. A reputable roofing contractor can help you document damage properly for your insurance adjuster.
How long does a roof replacement take?+
Most single-family residential roof replacements can be completed in 1–3 days for a standard asphalt shingle system. Complex roofs, larger homes, or specialty materials may take longer. Weather delays are common, so ask your contractor about their scheduling practices.
What's the lifespan of a residential roof?+
Asphalt shingles last 20–30 years, architectural shingles 30–40 years, metal roofs 40–70 years, and tile roofs 50+ years with proper maintenance. If your roof is approaching the end of its lifespan, a proactive replacement avoids emergency repair costs down the road.
