How to Evaluate a Plumber Before Hiring

The difference between a great plumbing experience and a nightmare often comes down to the vetting process. Homeowners who take ten minutes to verify a few key credentials before signing anything dramatically reduce their risk of a bad outcome.

Start with licensing. Most states require plumbers to hold a state-issued license, and you can verify any license number through your state's contractor licensing board website. Licensing requirements exist to protect homeowners — don't skip this step even for smaller jobs.

Beyond licensing, look for verifiable insurance (general liability and workers' compensation), a physical business address (not just a phone number), verifiable Google reviews from real customers in your area, and a clear written estimate before any work begins. A contractor who resists providing any of these should not be hired.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost?+
Most plumbing jobs range from $175 for minor repairs to $4,500+ for major work like water line replacement. Emergency calls typically include a service fee of $150–$250 on top of parts and labor. Always ask for a written estimate before work begins.
How do I find a trustworthy local plumber?+
Look for licensed, insured plumbers with verifiable local reviews on Google Maps. Avoid anyone who won't provide a written estimate, asks for full payment upfront, or can't show proof of license. A local plumber with consistent 4.5+ star reviews is your safest bet.
What counts as a plumbing emergency?+
Burst or frozen pipes, complete drain failure, sewage backup into the home, no running water, and gas line issues connected to plumbing are all emergencies requiring immediate same-day service. Most licensed plumbers offer 24/7 emergency response for these situations.
How long does it take a plumber to arrive for an emergency?+
A true emergency plumber should arrive within 1–2 hours for critical issues. If a company quotes you 8–12 hours for a burst pipe, keep calling. Local plumbers typically have faster response times than large national chains.
Can I fix a plumbing leak myself?+
Minor drips from faucets and running toilets can often be DIY-repaired with basic parts from a hardware store. However, anything involving your main water line, sewer line, water heater, or pipe inside a wall should be handled by a licensed plumber to avoid voiding home insurance or worsening damage.
What license does a plumber need?+
Requirements vary by state, but most require plumbers to hold a state-issued license (journeyman or master plumber). Always ask to see their license number and verify it with your state's contractor licensing board before work begins.
