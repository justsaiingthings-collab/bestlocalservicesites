How to Evaluate a Cleaning Professional Before Hiring

The difference between a great home cleaning experience and a nightmare often comes down to the vetting process. Homeowners who take ten minutes to verify a few key credentials before signing anything dramatically reduce their risk of a bad outcome.

Start with licensing. Most states require cleaning professionals to hold a state-issued license, and you can verify any license number through your state's contractor licensing board website. Licensing requirements exist to protect homeowners — don't skip this step even for smaller jobs.

Beyond licensing, look for verifiable insurance (general liability and workers' compensation), a physical business address (not just a phone number), verifiable Google reviews from real customers in your area, and a clear written estimate before any work begins. A contractor who resists providing any of these should not be hired.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does home cleaning service cost?+
Standard recurring cleaning for a 3-bedroom home typically runs $120–$180 per visit for biweekly service. Deep cleans and first-time cleans cost more, usually $200–$400. Move-out cleans for larger homes can reach $400–$600. Most companies offer discounts for recurring bookings.
How do I find a trustworthy cleaning service?+
Look for companies that conduct background checks on all cleaners, have consistent positive reviews on Google, provide itemized service lists, and offer written quotes before the first visit. Ask directly whether you'll have the same cleaner each visit.
What's the difference between a standard clean and a deep clean?+
A standard clean covers routine maintenance — surfaces, floors, bathrooms, kitchen. A deep clean is more intensive and covers areas often skipped in routine cleans: inside appliances, baseboards, window sills, light fixtures, and detailed scrubbing. Deep cleans are recommended quarterly or for first-time service.
Should I be home when the cleaner comes?+
Most established cleaning services are comfortable with clients providing a key or code. However, many homeowners prefer to be home for the first few visits. Discuss your preference with the company upfront and confirm their key management and security protocols.
How often should I schedule professional cleaning?+
For most households, biweekly service strikes the best balance between cleanliness and cost. Households with children, pets, or frequent guests often prefer weekly service. Monthly deep cleans are popular as a supplement to regular DIY maintenance.
